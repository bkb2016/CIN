My Journey

Information-1

I was born in a village in the Jalpaiguri district of North Bengal, India. I completed my BSc (2014) in Chemistry at Ananda Chandra College, Jalpaiguri, and my MSc (2016) at the University of North Bengal, Siliguri. I then pursued my PhD (12/2016 to 03/2023) at IISER Kolkata under the supervision of Dr. Ratheesh K. Vijayaraghavan, where I explored various aspects of organic optoelectronic devices.

Information-2

I. Design and synthesis of small donor–acceptor-type organic molecules for use as active layers and carrier transport layers in organic light-emitting devices. Their photophysical and electrochemical properties were investigated, along with theoretical calculations, including molecular orbitals, electrostatic potential (ESP) surfaces, singlet and triplet energy levels, transition dipole moments, electron affinity (EA), and oscillator strengths.

II. Thin film coating with a spin coater in the solution processing method. Metal such as Al, Au, Ag, and Cr deposition on different types of substrate (Glass, PET, Si-wafer, etc.) with the help of a thermal evaporator under very low pressure.

III. Fabrication of OFET device, I-V characterization of OFET device using probe station. Analysis of output curve, input curve, and mobility calculation.

IV. Material synthesis for the active layer of a resistive memory device, photophysical characterization, electrochemical characterization, RRAM device fabrication and characterization, single carrier device fabrication and characterization, vertical mobility calculation using the sandwich device, retention test, thickness dependence experiment, humidity dependence experiment, temperature dependence I-V experiment, Impedance experiment, thin film XRD, AFM, SEM, TEM experiment. Mechanism investigation for the switching of the RRAM device.

V. Organic resonator for photonic integrated circuits (PICs) application.
