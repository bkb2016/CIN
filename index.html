My Journey

Information-1

I was born in a village in the Jalpaiguri district of North Bengal, India. I completed my BSc (2014) in Chemistry at Ananda Chandra College, Jalpaiguri, and my MSc (2016) at the University of North Bengal, Siliguri. I then pursued my PhD (12/2016 to 03/2023) at IISER Kolkata under the supervision of Dr. Ratheesh K. Vijayaraghavan, where I explored various aspects of organic optoelectronic devices.

Research Highlights



Cutting-Edge Device Fabrication (7 Years' Expertise)

  • Solution-processed fabrication methods are used to create large-area devices. This highly efficient and scalable approach is crucial for producing a range of advanced devices, including:
    • Memristors: Devices that change their resistance based on the history of current flow, which can be used for non-volatile memory and neuromorphic computing.
    • Schottky Diodes: Semiconductor devices with a metal-semiconductor junction, utilized in rectifiers and high-frequency applications.
    • Organic Field-Effect Transistors (OFETs): Key components in flexible electronics, displays, and sensors, leveraging organic semiconductors.
    • Organic Light-Emitting Diodes (OLEDs): Essential for high-contrast, energy-efficient displays and lighting, known for their vibrant colors and thin profiles.
  • This domain involves seven years of experience, from January 2018 to the present.

Advanced Electrical Characterization & Analysis (5+ Years' Mastery)

  • A suite of sophisticated electrical characterization tools can be operated to assess the performance of electronic devices. These include:
    • Probe-station (Lakeshore): Can be used for precise electrical measurements on devices, often at controlled temperatures or environments.
    • Keithley 4200A-SCS Parameter Analyzer: Helps characterize semiconductor devices by measuring their current-voltage (I-V) and capacitance-voltage (C-V) characteristics.
    • KEITHLEY (2450 Source Meter): Is utilized for various electrical tests, combining a precision power supply and a high-accuracy digital multimeter.
    • Bio-Logic VSP-300 Potentiostat: Typically used for electrochemical measurements, which can be critical for characterizing charge transport and interfaces in certain device types.
  • The interpretation of electrical data from various devices allows for insights into:
    • I-V Characteristics of Resistive Memory Devices: Can be analyzed to understand their switching behavior and retention.
    • OFET Devices: Their performance parameters such as threshold voltage, on/off ratio, and subthreshold swing can be characterized.
    • Single Carrier Devices: Are essential for understanding charge transport mechanisms in materials, including Mobility calculation (how quickly carriers move through a material) and Space Charge Limited Conduction (SCLC) analysis (a model describing current flow limited by space charge effects).
  • Beyond electrical characterization, data from a wide array of structural and compositional analysis techniques can be interpreted to provide information on:
    • Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM): Provides high-resolution surface morphology imaging.
    • Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM): Offers highly magnified internal structure and elemental analysis.
    • Thin Film XRD (X-ray Diffraction): Is used to determine crystal structure and phase composition of thin films.
    • NMR (Nuclear Magnetic Resonance): Helps in molecular structure elucidation.
    • HRMS (High-Resolution Mass Spectrometry): Provides precise molecular weight determination and elemental composition.
    • IR Spectra (Infrared Spectroscopy): Is used for identifying functional groups in molecules.
    • DSC (Differential Scanning Calorimetry): Can be utilized for analyzing thermal transitions like melting and crystallization.
    • TGA (Thermogravimetric Analysis): Measures weight loss as a function of temperature, indicating thermal stability and composition.
  • This comprehensive electrical characterization and data analysis experience spans five years, from January 2018 to March 2023.

State-of-the-Art Instrumentation & Technical Proficiency (6+ Years' Hands-on)

  • A broad spectrum of advanced scientific instruments is utilized for materials synthesis, device fabrication, and characterization, including:
    • Thermal Evaporator (HHV): Is used for depositing thin films of materials by heating them in a vacuum.
    • Spin Coater: A common tool for creating uniform thin films on substrates by rapidly rotating them.
    • Ozone Cleaner: Used for surface treatment to remove organic contaminants and improve surface energy.
    • Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM): Specifically, NT-MDT Spectrum Instruments and WITec alpha300 A Atomic Force Microscopes can provide high-resolution topological images and material property mapping at the nanoscale.

    • Fluorescence Spectroscopy: With the Horiba Jobin Yvon Fluoromax-3, the emission of light from samples can be analyzed, providing insights into electronic structure and photophysical properties.
    • Confocal Micro Raman Spectrometer: A powerful tool for material identification, stress analysis, and crystallinity assessment by analyzing scattered light.
    • Cyclic Voltammetry (CH Instruments CHI604D): An electrochemical technique used to study redox properties of materials.
    • Polarizing Microscopes (ECLIPSE LV100N POL): Are used to observe anisotropic materials and their optical properties, often critical for crystal structures and liquid crystals.
  • This extensive practical experience covers six years, from January 2018 to November 2024.

Deep Theoretical and Modeling Expertise

  • A strong theoretical foundation is applied to understand material properties and predict their behavior, encompassing various advanced concepts:
    • Crystal Phase: Helps in understanding the arrangement of atoms in crystalline solids.
    • MC Carrier Mobility: Monte Carlo simulations are used for carrier mobility, describing how efficiently charge carriers move through a material.
    • Adsorption Energy: Refers to the energy change when molecules or atoms bind to a surface.
    • Geometry Optimization: Is used for finding the most stable molecular structure.
    • NBO Analysis (Natural Bond Orbital): Allows for analyzing electron delocalization and bonding.
    • NTO Analysis (Natural Transition Orbital): Provides understanding of electron transitions in excited states.
    • Dipole Moment Calculation: Determines the charge distribution within a molecule.
    • Oscillator Strength: Measures the probability of an electronic transition.
    • HOMO (Highest Occupied Molecular Orbital) & LUMO (Lowest Unoccupied Molecular Orbital): Are crucial for understanding electronic properties, reactivity, and optical absorption.
    • ESP Surface (Electrostatic Potential Surface): Is used for visualizing the charge distribution around a molecule.
    • Photoelectron Spectra: Can be analyzed to understand electronic structure through electron binding energies.
    • Band Diagram: Visualizes energy levels in semiconductors and insulators.
    • pka: A measure of acid strength.
    • Hirshfeld Surface: A tool for visualizing intermolecular interactions in crystals.
    • Interaction Energy: Quantifies the strength of intermolecular forces.
    • Energy Framework: Analyzes the contributions of different intermolecular interactions to crystal packing stability.
    • Raman Spectra: Helps in interpreting vibrational modes of molecules.
    • Hyperpolarizability: A measure of a material's non-linear optical response.
    • Molecular Dynamics: Involves simulating the physical movements of atoms and molecules over time.
    • NCI (Non-Covalent Interactions): Is used for identifying and visualizing weak interactions in molecules and complexes.

Versatile Synthetic Chemistry & Materials Development (7 Years' Dedicated Practice)

  • Fundamental organic synthesis, including basic one-step organic synthesis, is performed. This is complemented by thorough product analysis where a range of spectroscopic and diffraction techniques are employed:
    • NMR (1H & 13C): Provides detailed structural characterization of organic compounds.
    • Infrared Spectroscopy: Is used to identify functional groups.
    • HRMS (High-Resolution Mass Spectrometry): Verifies precise molecular weight and elemental composition.
    • Single crystal XRD (X-ray Diffraction): Determines the exact atomic arrangement in crystalline solids.
  • Essential purification processes are applied to ensure the purity of synthesized materials:
    • Column Chromatography: A standard laboratory technique for separating compounds based on their differential partitioning between a stationary phase and a mobile phase.
    • Thin Layer Chromatography (TLC): A quick and qualitative method for monitoring reactions and assessing purity.
    • Rotary Evaporator: Is used for efficient solvent removal under reduced pressure.
  • Practical skills extend to solvent distillation, which ensures the purity of common laboratory solvents like DCM (dichloromethane), Toluene, and DMF (dimethylformamide) for sensitive reactions.
  • Inorganic synthesis is performed for developing various nanomaterials with diverse applications, such as:
    • ZnO nanoparticles: Zinc oxide nanoparticles, known for their semiconducting and UV-blocking properties.
    • Au nanorods: Gold nanorods, used in plasmonics, sensing, and biomedical applications.
    • Ag nanorods: Silver nanorods, valued for their antimicrobial properties and optical applications.
    • CdS nanoparticles: Cadmium sulfide nanoparticles, important in quantum dots and optoelectronics.
    • Perovskite (CsPbBr3): Cesium lead bromide perovskite, a material of significant interest for solar cells and LEDs due to its excellent optoelectronic properties.
  • This comprehensive synthetic experience spans seven years, from January 2017 to the present.
