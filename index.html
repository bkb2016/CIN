My Journey

Information-1

I was born in a village in the Jalpaiguri district of North Bengal, India. I completed my BSc (2014) in Chemistry at Ananda Chandra College, Jalpaiguri, and my MSc (2016) at the University of North Bengal, Siliguri. There, I explored the field of organic nanophotonics and gained experience in organic resonators, fabricating Ru-based OLEDs, and creating photonic devices using FIB milling.

1. Cutting-Edge Device Fabrication (7 Years' Expertise)

I specialize in solution-processed fabrication methods for creating large-area devices, a highly efficient and scalable approach crucial for advanced electronics. My extensive experience ensures the production of a diverse range of high-performance devices:

  • RRAM (Memristors): Devices designed for non-volatile memory and neuromorphic computing applications, capable of changing resistance based on external stimuli.
  • Schottky Diodes: Essential semiconductor devices with a metal-semiconductor-metal structure, utilized in current rectifiers and high-frequency applications.
  • Organic Field-Effect Transistors (OFETs): Key components leveraging organic semiconductors for flexible electronics, displays, and sensors.
  • Organic Light-Emitting Diodes (OLEDs): Fundamental for high-contrast, energy-efficient displays and lighting, celebrated for their vibrant colors and thin profiles.

Experience: This domain involves seven years of dedicated practice, from January 2018 to the present.

2.üìä Advanced Electrical Characterization & Data Analysis (5+ Years' Mastery)

My mastery in assessing electronic device performance relies on a collection of sophisticated electrical characterization tools and insightful data interpretation. I analyze complex electrical data to gain deep insights into device behavior:

  • Instrumentation Proficiency:

6.üß† Deep Theoretical and Modeling Expertise

A strong theoretical foundation underpins my work, enabling me to understand material properties and predict their behavior through advanced concepts and simulations:

  • Molecular & Electronic Structure:
    • Geometry Optimization: For finding the most stable molecular structure.
    • HOMO (Highest Occupied Molecular Orbital) & LUMO (Lowest Unoccupied Molecular Orbital): Crucial for understanding electronic properties, reactivity, and optical absorption.
    • ESP Surface (Electrostatic Potential Surface): For visualizing the charge distribution around a molecule.
    • Dipole Moment Calculation: Determines the charge distribution within a molecule (Ground State and Excited State).
    • NBO Analysis (Natural Bond Orbital): Allows for analyzing electron delocalization and bonding.
    • NTO Analysis (Natural Transition Orbital): Provides understanding of electron transitions in excited states.
    • Oscillator Strength: Measures the probability of an electronic transition.
    • Photoelectron Spectra: To understand electronic structure through electron binding energies.
    • pka: A measure of acid strength.
  • Charge Transport & Solid State:
    • MC Carrier Mobility: Monte Carlo simulations describing how efficiently charge carriers move through a material.
    • Crystal Morphology: Helps understand the arrangement of planes in crystalline solids.
    • Band Diagram: Visualizes energy levels in semiconductors and insulators.
  • Intermolecular Interactions & Dynamics:
    • Adsorption Energy: Refers to the energy change when molecules or atoms bind to a surface.
    • Hirshfeld Surface: A tool for visualizing intermolecular interactions in crystals.
    • Interaction Energy: Quantifies the strength of intermolecular forces.
    • Energy Framework: Analyzes the contributions of different intermolecular interactions to crystal packing stability.
    • Molecular Dynamics: Involves simulating the physical movements of atoms and molecules over time.
    • NCI (Non-Covalent Interactions): Used for identifying and visualizing weak interactions in molecules and complexes.
  • Spectroscopic Interpretation:
    • Raman Spectra: Helps interpret vibrational modes of molecules.
    • Hyperpolarizability: A measure of a material's non-linear optical response.
